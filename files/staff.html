Administration

Nikki Scheele

Director, BS Kinesiology, MSW

Nikki’s early education experience spans 15 years. She began teaching at WUNS in 2009. Prior to that, she worked as a service coordinator for Special School District for six years, providing education and support for parents of children with special needs. She also served as a social worker in Collinsville, Illinois, for three years.

Nikki earned her Bachelor of Science degree in kinesiology from the University of Michigan and her Master of Social Work from Washington University in St. Louis. Inspired by her first science teacher, she loves to teach children about science and nature.

She enjoys seeing preschoolers explore and discover the wonders in their world. WUNS is a special place to work and teach, Nikki believes, because the dedicated staff understands the unique needs of children. In addition, the campus location makes for special opportunities for children to engage with a higher education environment.

Nikki, originally from Michigan, has been married to her best friend, Chris Scheele, for 21 years, and they call University City home. They have two children—son Nick and daughter Parker, both WUNS alums. In her free time, Nikki loves to spend time with family and neighbors. Her home is known for its get-togethers, particularly postseason Cardinal games. Travel is also a favorite hobby; her family has visited the Caribbean, Mexico, Brazil and Europe and looks forward to new travel adventures every summer.

Amber Ott

Amber has worked at the Nursery School since 2002. She attained her Bachelor of Science in education at the University of Kansas (Go Jawhawks!) and her Master of Arts in education from the University of South Florida.

She has taught middle school children in Iceland, Florida, California and St. Louis. Amber and her husband have three children—daughter Taylor, and sons Max and Cade. All three children attended preschool at WUNS.

She believes that the amazing teachers and families make WUNS a special place to work. Amber’s administrative skills help keep all the day-to-day operations of the school running smoothly. She also coordinates the student workers’ schedules and the parent association activities. In her spare time, Amber enjoys tennis and reading.

Teachers

Chona Carson

AB Physchology, MAEd

Chona is beginning her 13th year as a teacher in the Nursery School. She teams with Marbie in the Teddy Bear classroom and Marsha in the Panda Bear classroom.

Chona has over 20 years of experience in the early childhood education field. Her degrees include a Bachelor of Arts in psychology from the University of Kansas and a Master of Arts in teaching from Webster University. Chona’s favorite times with the WUNS children include art activities—she enjoys watching them create using different media. Circle time is another favorite of hers, when children enjoy listening to stories and predicting what happens next, as well as singing and counting to teacher-led prompts. For Chona, WUNS is a very special place to teach because of the diverse families that enroll. She also appreciates working with her teammates and collaborating with the other teachers on staff.

Chona is married with two children in college. During her free time, she loves to spend time with her family, read, garden and walk her dogs.

Marsha Graeber

BS Parks and Recreation

Marsha Graeber has taught at WUNS for 20 years. She currently works with the Bear Cubs in the morning and the Panda Bears in the afternoon. Marsha’s enthusiasm in the classroom is contagious. Her greatest joy in working with children is to provide a positive environment to support self-discovery, independence, resilience, confidence, curiosity, self-regulation and a strong self-esteem. She treasures all the relationships with children and their families cultivated over the years. Marsha believes that WUNS is a special place to teach because of the talented, respectful staff, the unique experiences of the children, and the open communication that the school community supports.

Her Bachelor of Science degree in outdoor parks and recreation was attained at the University of Missouri in Columbia.

Marsha is married to husband Ken; they have two daughters, Emily and Sarah. The whole family appreciates being active in the outdoors, enjoying running, golf, biking, hiking and working in the garden. The Graebers are also avid Cardinal baseball fans!

Heather Huewe

AB Speech Communication,
MAT Early Childhood Education

Heather Huewe is thrilled to begin her third year teaching at WUNS. After staying home for several years to raise four daughters, Heather eagerly returned to the classroom and found WUNS to be a wonderful fit. Heather was drawn to WUNS for its warm, family environment, its collegiality, its professionalism, and the opportunity to work not only with young children but also with young adults from Washington University in St. Louis. Prior to having her daughters, Heather taught first, sixth, and eighth grade over the course of twelve years at MICDS, and developed a love for each age group. She teams with Suzy in the morning Big Bears.

Heather earned her BA in Speech and Communications from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill (go Tarheels!) and an MAT in Early Childhood Education from Emory University.

A devoted runner for her entire adult life, she will begin coaching her tenth season of Girls on the Run this fall, and has completed several marathons for charity. Heather is most proud of coordinating the annual Race for Refugees, which benefits the International Institute of St. Louis. This venture combines her love of running with her belief in giving back to our shared community. In her free time Heather loves to stay active and get outside with her kind husband – and fellow educator – Patrick, their four unique daughters Rosie, Grace, Anna, and Mary Catherine, and crazy pup Charlie. Together they like to walk to their friendly Olivette neighborhood Starbucks, ride bikes to the bookstore (more coffee!), go to the zoo, play tennis and soccer, and of course run. Heather also loves to read, bake, keep up with current events, and stay connected with friends, family, and neighbors (a.k.a. talk…just ask her family!).

Katie Loynd

BS Elementary Education, MAEd Teaching,
Early Childhood Education

Katie is very excited to begin her fifth year at WUNS, in the 2019 – 2020 school year teaching with Chona in the Teddy Bear classroom (mornings) and Colleen in the Sun Bear classroom (afternoons). She has taught kindergarten, first and second grades in the Ladue School District for many years.

She attained her undergraduate degree in elementary education from Creighton University and her Master of Arts in teaching early education from Webster University. Katie is inspired by young children; she is constantly amazed by how they see the joy in simple things. One of her goals as a teacher is to provide an enriching environment that naturally fosters curiosity, engaging conversations, imaginative play, creative movement, and thoughtful expression. She believes WUNS is a special place because of its warm and welcoming energy and its unique location on a college campus.

Katie and her husband Mike are St. Louis natives. They have four children, Alli (21), Jake (19), Elizabeth (17) and Will (14) and a dog Murphy, “the best West Highland White Terrier ever.” In her free time, Katie enjoys hiking, travel, swimming and being with her family.

Miriam Rotskoff

BA, Spanish Literature and Elementary Education,
MAEd in Curriculum and Instruction

Miriam is thrilled to join the WUNS community this year as a teacher in the Bear Cub classroom. Having previously worked with the Bear Cubs during summer camp, she is excited to be back at the nursery school and working with such a dedicated and warm group of teachers, students and families.

Miriam attended the University of Colorado-Boulder, where she earned BA degrees in Spanish Literature and Elementary Education, as well as her MAEd in Curriculum and Instruction with an emphasis on Literacy, Language and Culture. She spent seven years teaching elementary school in Denver, at a private bilingual school in Mexico and with the School District of Clayton.

After spending time at home with her three children, Miriam began substitute teaching at area preschools and she most recently taught preschool at New City School. She loves working with preschool-aged children because of their unique opportunity to learn through play, creative expression and exploration.

Miriam and her husband have two daughters and a son. When she is not busy with her family, she loves spending time with friends, reading and travelling home to the mountains of Colorado.

Becky Craig

Science Specialist, Naturalist,
Bachelor of Science in Early Childhood Education

Becky is thrilled to continue the Nature on the Move program that has been coming to WUNS for 19 years. Becky holds a Bachelor of Science in Early Childhood Education from the University of Missouri-Columbia. She has taught young children for a total of 18 years, 6 in kindergarten and more recently 12 in Pre-K. Becky and her husband Randy have been married for 26 years and have four children, all currently in college. Becky and Randy love to spend time on their property in Northeastern Missouri near Mark Twain Lake where they enjoy watching and listening to all the wildlife, fishing, boating, and riding their horses. Becky believes that one way to preserve our natural resources is to teach the youngest members of our society to love and respect the natural world around them. Nature on the Move allows children to get up close to Missouri’s wildlife and learn how each living thing affects the world in which they live.

Christina Ryan

BA Music, EdM Early Childhood Education,
Certified Music Together Teacher

I am delighted to be leading music classes at WUNS beginning this year! I bring experiences as a musician, educator, and parent. I taught kindergarten for 12 years, am a certified Music Together teacher, have taught flute lessons, and have played in and directed a variety of ensembles. I continue to play flute professionally, but I have greatly missed the special energy that lights up when I share music and movement with young children, who have an innate desire to make playful noise and wiggle their bodies. I am very excited to share that joy with all the bears at WUNS…including my own little boy who is a Big Bear and Sunbear.

I have been married to my high school sweetheart, Stephen, for 18 years. In addition to our son, Brendon, we have our little ladybug, Isabelle. When I’m not trying to keep up with my littles, I enjoy volunteering with our church, neighborhood groups, and—of course!—at WUNS. I also love to read, play flute and piano, do anything artsy and crafty, and go out to eat at restaurants.
